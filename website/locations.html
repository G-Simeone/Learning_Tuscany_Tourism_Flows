Location Clustering Methodology

In the following plots we display the findings from the location clustering method applied to all Tuscany’s municipalities. What emerges from the results are groups of municipalities which were commonly visited during the same trip. Every group has its own colour and, by hovering over the municipality, a window will pop up showing the name of the municipality, the cluster number it belongs to, and a list of the other municipalities which were most visited during the same trip (from the most co-visited to the least co-visited). Some municipalities in the map will appear white, this is because the cluster they belong to is too small (fewer than 5 municipalities), and we decided to exclude those from the colouring. Moreover, you will see a few isolated municipalities having a different color than their surrounding cluster. These are generally municipalities that belonged “nowhere” (i.e. they were not visited with their adjacent municipalities), and where treated by the algorithm together as the “odd ones out”. In both cases (white or isolated municipalities) this might happen because either there was no cell tower in the municipality (hence no signalling from the tourists’ phones were collected from the municipality) or, simply, no tourist visited those municipalities during that season.

We performed the clustering analyses on all tourists who visited Tuscany during summer, post-summer and winter. Select season from the drop-down menu below to view the graphical displays of each clustering analysis followed by descriptions.
